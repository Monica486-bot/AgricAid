About AgriAid

AgriAid is a platform designed to assist farmers in understanding & providing essential farming methods, seeds, tools, and practices through hands-on workshops and in-person support focused on improving community agricultural outcomes.

100+ Farmers Connected
1 Year Established

Our Purpose

Driving agricultural innovation and sustainability in refugee communities

Our Mission

To provide innovative, accessible, and sustainable agricultural solutions that empower farmers in Rhino Refugee Camp to achieve food security, improve livelihoods, and build resilient communities through technology-driven farming practices.

Our Vision

A world where every farmer, regardless of their circumstances, has access to modern agricultural tools and knowledge to cultivate thriving, sustainable farms that feed communities and preserve the environment for future generations.

Our Values

  • Innovation: Embracing cutting-edge technology
  • Sustainability: Protecting our environment
  • Community: Building stronger connections
  • Empowerment: Enabling farmer success
  • Accessibility: Making tools available to all

What We Do

Comprehensive agricultural solutions for modern farming

Community Data Collection

Field workshop participants and community volunteers collect simple, actionable observations about soil health, moisture, and crop conditions. This information is used for planning, group learning, and to track improvements over time.

Organic Manure (Yield) increase

We help farmers boost yields by promoting the use of crop rotation, mulching, organic manure and sustainable practices, improving soil fertility and crop productivity for healthier harvests.

Water Management

Efficient irrigation systems and water conservation techniques designed to optimize water usage and reduce environmental impact.

Farmer Education

Comprehensive training programs, workshops, and digital resources to enhance farming knowledge and technical skills.

Community Building

Connecting farmers through resource sharing platforms, collaborative tools, and community-driven agricultural initiatives.
